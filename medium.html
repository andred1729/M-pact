2 months before collision:

With only months left until collision, options for mitigation become more limited. There is only one real prevention strategy commonly discussed in planetary defense research...

Nuclear explosive:

This method is exactly what it sounds like—launch a nuke at the meteor. The energy deposited by the bomb heats the asteroid’s surface, vaporizing material and moving it in the opposite direction. Without the need to build a spacecraft from scratch, this method can be deployed within a relatively short timeframe.

This is most likely the most effective prevention technique, imparting 10-100 times more momentum to the asteroid than other methods. It's good in scenarios with little warning time, and can work on large objects.

However, there are international political complications. Implementation of a nuclear weapon in general would raise geopolitical tensions, and would likely set a bad precedent for future military actions in space. Also, many citizens fear and reject the idea of using nuclear weapons in any capacity.
